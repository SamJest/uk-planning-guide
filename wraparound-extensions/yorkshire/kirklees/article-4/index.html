Article 4 Restrictions for Wraparound Extension Projects

Understanding article 4 restrictions is important when planning a wraparound extension project in Kirklees. National planning rules set limits on size, height and location of developments that may be built under permitted development rights.

Local planning policies, conservation areas and Article 4 directions may also affect whether planning permission is required.

Wraparound Extension Planning Rules in Kirklees vs National Permitted Development

UK permitted development legislation allows many wraparound extension projects without planning permission provided certain limits are followed.

However local planning authorities such as those covering Kirklees may impose additional planning controls through conservation area policies or Article 4 directions.

Article 4 Restrictions Questions

Do I need planning permission for wraparound extension projects in Kirklees?

Many wraparound extension developments in Kirklees may fall under permitted development rights if the project remains within national planning limits.

What height limits apply to wraparound extension developments?

Outbuildings with pitched roofs may normally reach up to 4 metres in height, while flat roof structures are typically limited to 3 metres.

Do conservation areas affect wraparound extension planning rules?

Yes. Additional planning restrictions may apply within conservation areas or where Article 4 directions remove permitted development rights.
