Wraparound Extension Boundary Rules in Westminster

Understanding boundary rules is important when planning a wraparound extension in Westminster.

Boundary Distance Calculator

Boundary Distance Guide

Boundary rules determine whether a wraparound extension can be built without planning permission.

  • Within 2m of a boundary → max height usually 2.5m
  • Further than 2m from boundary → taller structures may be allowed
  • Design and roof shape may also affect permitted development rules

Local Planning Context in Westminster

Planning rules affecting wraparound extension developments in Westminster may vary depending on local planning policies, conservation areas and Article 4 directions.

The local planning authority covering Westminster may apply additional controls in certain neighbourhoods to protect historic character or residential amenity.

Homeowners planning a wraparound extension project in Westminster should review local council planning guidance before starting work.

Frequently Asked Questions

How close to a boundary can a wraparound extension be built?

A wraparound extension can usually be built close to a boundary, but the height is typically limited to 2.5 metres if the structure is within 2 metres of the boundary.

Do boundary rules affect permitted development rights?

Yes. The location of the structure relative to boundaries determines which height limits apply under permitted development rules.

Do neighbours need to approve a wraparound extension?

Neighbour approval is not usually required under permitted development, but developments must still comply with planning rules and building regulations.
