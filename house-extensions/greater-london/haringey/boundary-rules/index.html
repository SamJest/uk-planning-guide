House Extension Boundary Rules in Haringey

This guide explains boundary rules affecting house extension projects in Haringey, Greater London.

Boundary Rules

Side extensions must be single storey, no wider than half the width of the original house, and must not extend beyond the principal elevation.

Boundary distance rules help protect neighbouring properties from overshadowing, overlooking, and overbearing development. Structures built very close to boundaries are subject to stricter height limits to minimise their visual impact.

Height Rules

Single-storey rear extensions must not exceed 4m in height. Eaves height must not exceed 3m if within 2m of a boundary.

Height limits exist to prevent extensions or roof alterations from overpowering neighbouring properties or significantly changing the character of the surrounding area. Planning officers typically assess whether the proposed structure would appear dominant or intrusive when viewed from neighbouring homes or public spaces.

Even where a development falls within permitted development limits, larger structures may still require careful design to avoid overlooking or overshadowing nearby properties.

Depth Rules

Rear extensions can extend up to 4m for detached houses or 3m for semi-detached and terraced houses under standard permitted development rules.

Depth limits restrict how far an extension can project from the original rear wall of the property. These rules help ensure that extensions remain proportionate to the original house and do not create excessive loss of light or privacy for neighbouring homes.

Boundary Rules for House Extension Projects

Understanding boundary rules is important when planning a house extension project in Haringey. National planning rules set limits on size, height and location of developments that may be built under permitted development rights.

Local planning policies, conservation areas and Article 4 directions may also affect whether planning permission is required.

House Extension Planning Rules in Haringey vs National Permitted Development

UK permitted development legislation allows many house extension projects without planning permission provided certain limits are followed.

However local planning authorities such as those covering Haringey may impose additional planning controls through conservation area policies or Article 4 directions.

Boundary Rules Questions

Do I need planning permission for house extension projects in Haringey?

Many house extension developments in Haringey may fall under permitted development rights if the project remains within national planning limits.

What height limits apply to house extension developments?

Outbuildings with pitched roofs may normally reach up to 4 metres in height, while flat roof structures are typically limited to 3 metres.

Do conservation areas affect house extension planning rules?

Yes. Additional planning restrictions may apply within conservation areas or where Article 4 directions remove permitted development rights.
