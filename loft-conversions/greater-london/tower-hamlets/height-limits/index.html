Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum height for a loft conversion?

Under permitted development rules, outbuildings are typically limited to 2.5 metres in height if they are within 2 metres of a boundary. Taller structures may be allowed further away from boundaries.

Can a loft conversion be 3 metres high?

Yes, but only if the structure is located more than 2 metres away from the boundary and meets other permitted development conditions.

Does roof type affect height limits?

Yes. Buildings with dual-pitched roofs may be allowed up to around 4 metres in height under permitted development rules.
