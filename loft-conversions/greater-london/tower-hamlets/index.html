Loft Conversion Planning Permission Rules in Tower Hamlets

Loft Conversion development in Tower Hamlets, Greater London typically follows UK permitted development rules, although local planning policies and conservation area restrictions may apply.

Homeowners considering a loft conversion should review local planning guidance carefully to ensure compliance with height limits, boundary rules and permitted development criteria.

The guidance below summarises the key planning considerations for properties located in Tower Hamlets.

Permitted Development

Loft conversions may be permitted development subject to limits and conditions set out in permitted development rights.

Volume Limit

Additional roof space must not exceed 40 cubic metres for terraced houses or 50 cubic metres for detached and semi-detached houses.

Principal Elevation Restriction

Roof extensions are not permitted on the principal elevation of a house that faces a highway.

Height Limit

The extension must not be higher than the highest part of the existing roof.

Eaves Alignment

The eaves of the extension must be no higher than the eaves of the existing roof.

Materials Match

Materials used for the exterior of the loft conversion should be similar in appearance to those used on the existing house.

Side Facing Windows

Any side-facing windows must be obscure-glazed and non-opening unless the opening parts are more than 1.7 metres above the floor level.

Balconies Restriction

Verandas, balconies and raised platforms are not permitted development.

Roof Enlargement Location

Roof enlargements must be set back at least 20 centimetres from the original eaves measured along the roof plane.

Side Roof Extensions

Side-facing roof extensions are not permitted development.

Loft Conversion Planning Permission Planning Checklist

Before starting a loft conversion project in Tower Hamlets, homeowners should check the following planning considerations:

  • Whether the development falls within permitted development rules
  • Whether the property is located in a conservation area
  • Whether Article 4 directions remove permitted development rights
  • Whether the property is a listed building
  • Whether building regulations approval is required
  • Whether the design complies with local planning policies

Common Reasons Loft Conversion Planning Permission Applications Are Refused

Planning applications for loft conversions in Tower Hamlets may be refused for a variety of reasons depending on the design of the development and local planning policies.

  • The development exceeds permitted development size limits
  • The design is considered out of character with neighbouring properties
  • The proposal causes overlooking or loss of privacy
  • The development results in overshadowing or loss of light
  • The property is located in a conservation area with additional restrictions
  • An Article 4 direction removes permitted development rights

Planning Permission FAQs

Do I need planning permission for a loft conversion in Tower Hamlets?

Many loft conversion projects in Tower Hamlets can be carried out under permitted development rights in England. However planning permission may still be required depending on the scale of the development, the design of the project and whether the property is affected by conservation area policies or Article 4 directions.

What planning rules apply to loft conversion projects in Tower Hamlets?

Development in Tower Hamlets must comply with national permitted development rules as well as any local planning policies set by the council. These rules may control height, depth, roof alterations, materials and the position of the development on the property.

How much roof space can be added in a loft conversion?

Permitted development rules place limits on the amount of additional roof space that can be created and restrict extensions above the existing roof ridge. Dormers and roof alterations must also comply with national planning rules.

Do conservation areas affect loft conversion projects in Tower Hamlets?

Properties located in conservation areas within Tower Hamlets may be subject to additional planning controls designed to preserve the character of the area. Development that would normally fall under permitted development may require planning permission.

Can Article 4 directions affect loft conversion projects in Tower Hamlets?

Some councils introduce Article 4 directions which remove permitted development rights in certain areas. Where an Article 4 direction applies, planning permission may be required for development that would normally be allowed under national rules.

Do listed buildings need permission for a loft conversion?

Listed building consent is normally required for alterations or extensions to listed buildings even where the work might otherwise fall under permitted development rights.

Where can I check official planning guidance for loft conversion projects in Tower Hamlets?

Planning guidance for loft conversion projects in Tower Hamlets can usually be found on the official council planning pages, national Planning Portal guidance and local planning policy documents published by the authority.
