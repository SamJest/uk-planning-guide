UK Local Planning Authorities

Use this section when the local authority layer is the real source of uncertainty, especially for conservation areas, heritage controls, Article 4 directions or council-specific guidance.

What this hub is for

It helps you move from a broad planning question into the right planning area, the right council and the most useful local project page quickly.

What it does not replace

It does not replace the local planning authority, site-specific design review or formal confirmation where the proposal is close to a limit.

Decision guide

When The Council Hub Is The Right Starting Point And When It Is Not

Best when

  • The local authority layer is the main uncertainty.
  • You want to compare planning areas before choosing one council path.
  • Conservation areas, Article 4 or heritage controls may be the real blocker.

Go elsewhere first when

  • You already know the exact project type and need the answer-first local project page.
  • The question is still broad enough that a tool or FAQ would narrow it faster.
  • You need a site-specific route decision rather than a navigation layer.

What usually settles it faster

  • Pick the planning area first, then the council, then the matching project or rule page.
  • Use a tool first if the route is still broad and the local authority is not yet clear.
  • Use the local authority page alongside the project guide when local controls may change the answer.
