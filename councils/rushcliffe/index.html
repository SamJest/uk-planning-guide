Driveway in Rushcliffe

In Rushcliffe, most house driveways can proceed without a planning application where the work stays inside the curtilage and front-of-house surfacing over 5 square metres is permeable or drains to a porous area within the site. Forming or widening the access across a verge or footway is separate and can still need highway consent.

House Extension in Rushcliffe

Some house extensions can be permitted development in England under Class A, but the route depends on whether the proposal is rear, side or two storey, how much of the original curtilage remains and whether any local restriction removes PD rights. Councils usually focus on whether the work stays subordinate to the original building and avoids an overbearing effect on neighbours.

Loft Conversion in Rushcliffe

A loft conversion can fall within Class B permitted development in England if the roof enlargement stays within the volume allowance, does not project beyond the principal roof slope facing a highway and keeps to the Class B conditions. Councils usually focus on whether the work stays subordinate to the original building and avoids an overbearing effect on neighbours.

Outbuildings in Rushcliffe

A detached outbuilding can usually stay within householder permitted development if it is for a purpose incidental to the house, sits behind the principal elevation and meets the Class E height and coverage limits. That route does not cover a separate dwelling or self-contained annexe.
