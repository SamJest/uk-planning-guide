Garden Room Planning Permission in Harrow

Planning guidance for garden room planning permission in Harrow, Greater London.

Permitted Development

Outbuildings may be permitted development subject to national permitted development limits and restrictions.

Max Height

Maximum overall height of 4 metres with a dual-pitched roof, or 3 metres for any other roof; reduced to 2.5 metres if within 2 metres of a boundary.

Max Eaves Height

Maximum eaves height of 2.5 metres.

Max Total Coverage

Outbuildings and extensions must not cover more than 50% of the total area of land around the original house.

Boundary Rules

No verandas, balconies or raised platforms are permitted under permitted development rights.

Distance From Boundary Rule

Maximum height limited to 2.5 metres if within 2 metres of a boundary.

Common Reasons Garden Room Planning Permission Applications Are Refused

Planning applications for garden rooms in Harrow may be refused for a variety of reasons depending on the design of the development and local planning policies.

  • The development exceeds permitted development size limits
  • The design is considered out of character with neighbouring properties
  • The proposal causes overlooking or loss of privacy
  • The development results in overshadowing or loss of light
  • The property is located in a conservation area with additional restrictions
  • An Article 4 direction removes permitted development rights

Garden Room Planning Permission Planning Checklist

Before starting a garden room project in Harrow, homeowners should check the following planning considerations:

  • Whether the development falls within permitted development rules
  • Whether the property is located in a conservation area
  • Whether Article 4 directions remove permitted development rights
  • Whether the property is a listed building
  • Whether building regulations approval is required
  • Whether the design complies with local planning policies
