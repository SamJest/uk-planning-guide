Garden Room Planning Permission Rules in City of London

Garden Room development in City of London, Greater London typically follows UK permitted development rules, although local planning policies and conservation area restrictions may apply.

Homeowners considering a garden room should review local planning guidance carefully to ensure compliance with height limits, boundary rules and permitted development criteria.

The guidance below summarises the key planning considerations for properties located in City of London.

Permitted Development

Outbuildings may be permitted development subject to national permitted development limits and restrictions.

Max Height

Maximum overall height of 4 metres with a dual-pitched roof, or 3 metres for any other roof; reduced to 2.5 metres if within 2 metres of a boundary.

Max Eaves Height

Maximum eaves height of 2.5 metres.

Max Total Coverage

Outbuildings and extensions must not cover more than 50% of the total area of land around the original house.

Boundary Rules

No verandas, balconies or raised platforms are permitted under permitted development rights.

Distance From Boundary Rule

Maximum height limited to 2.5 metres if within 2 metres of a boundary.

Garden Room Planning Permission Planning Checklist

Before starting a garden room project in City of London, homeowners should check the following planning considerations:

  • Whether the development falls within permitted development rules
  • Whether the property is located in a conservation area
  • Whether Article 4 directions remove permitted development rights
  • Whether the property is a listed building
  • Whether building regulations approval is required
  • Whether the design complies with local planning policies

Common Reasons Garden Room Planning Permission Applications Are Refused

Planning applications for garden rooms in City of London may be refused for a variety of reasons depending on the design of the development and local planning policies.

  • The development exceeds permitted development size limits
  • The design is considered out of character with neighbouring properties
  • The proposal causes overlooking or loss of privacy
  • The development results in overshadowing or loss of light
  • The property is located in a conservation area with additional restrictions
  • An Article 4 direction removes permitted development rights

Planning Permission FAQs

Do I need planning permission for a garden room in City of London?

Many garden room projects in City of London can be carried out under permitted development rights in England. However planning permission may still be required depending on the scale of the development, the design of the project and whether the property is affected by conservation area policies or Article 4 directions.

What planning rules apply to garden room projects in City of London?

Development in City of London must comply with national permitted development rules as well as any local planning policies set by the council. These rules may control height, depth, roof alterations, materials and the position of the development on the property.

What height can an outbuilding be without planning permission?

Outbuildings are normally limited to 4 metres with a dual pitched roof or 3 metres for other roof types. The maximum height is reduced to 2.5 metres if the structure is within 2 metres of a boundary.

Do conservation areas affect garden room projects in City of London?

Properties located in conservation areas within City of London may be subject to additional planning controls designed to preserve the character of the area. Development that would normally fall under permitted development may require planning permission.

Can Article 4 directions affect garden room projects in City of London?

Some councils introduce Article 4 directions which remove permitted development rights in certain areas. Where an Article 4 direction applies, planning permission may be required for development that would normally be allowed under national rules.

Do listed buildings need permission for a garden room?

Listed building consent is normally required for alterations or extensions to listed buildings even where the work might otherwise fall under permitted development rights.

Where can I check official planning guidance for garden room projects in City of London?

Planning guidance for garden room projects in City of London can usually be found on the official council planning pages, national Planning Portal guidance and local planning policy documents published by the authority.
